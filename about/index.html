Hi, I’m Maya Benari.

I’m a designer and front-end developer specializing in creating scalable, accessible design systems that empower teams to build intuitive, user-friendly experiences. With over 15 years of experience across government, healthcare, startups, and tech companies, I’ve honed my skills at the intersection of design and development.

Currently, I design large-scale systems at Dayforce, ensuring teams can move quickly and efficiently without sacrificing usability. My work centers on bridging design and engineering, fostering collaboration, and crafting flexible, future-proof systems that cater to diverse product needs.

Previously, I was a core member of the U.S. Web Design System team at 18F, where I helped evolve a foundational UI library into a widely adopted ecosystem supporting hundreds of federal agencies. I also served as a Code for America fellow, collaborating with the City of San Antonio to enhance public services through technology.

I believe great design is inclusive, scalable, and human-centered—enabling teams to build better products and create more meaningful experiences.
