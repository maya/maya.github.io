About

Systems as
decision infrastructure

Design systems aren't just UI — they're decision-making systems.

I work at the intersection of design, engineering, and systems thinking — building the structures that determine how products are made.

I've focused on design systems as infrastructure: not just component libraries, but systems that shape how organizations operate. Most recently, I led foundational design system work at a global HR and payroll platform, shaping component architecture, token systems, and contribution models used across multiple product teams. My role spanned design and front-end development, driving alignment between design intent and production reality.

Previously, I was a front-end lead on the U.S. Web Design System, where I built core components designed for decade-long durability — now serving 1B+ page views across hundreds of federal agencies.

I'm particularly interested in where systems are going next: AI-assisted workflows, tighter design-to-code pipelines, and new forms of system intelligence that make design logic explicit, traceable, and scalable.
